MARKET ANALYSIS:
56 Leonard commands a premium due to the Herzog & de Meuron design and Tribeca location. Lower-floor units trade at a slight discount to higher floors but the terrace on Unit 5N is a significant value-add (only 12 units in the building have private terraces).

Average $/SF for Tribeca luxury condos: $1,400-$1,700/SF. The asking price of $1,491/SF is reasonable given the terrace, double-height ceiling, and home office. The higher monthly carrying costs ($7,040 vs. $5,425 at One Park) reflect the more exclusive amenity program and Tribeca tax basis.

Negotiation target: $4,800,000-$4,950,000 ($1,404-$1,447/SF). The lower floor and higher carrying costs provide negotiation leverage.

KEY CONSIDERATION:
As an architect, the opportunity to live in a Herzog & de Meuron building may have professional value beyond the financial comparison. The building's construction detailing and material quality are exceptional and worth experiencing firsthand.
