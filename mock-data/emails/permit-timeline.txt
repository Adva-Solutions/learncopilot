Akshay,

Good timeline. A few things from my experience with Fort Lauderdale Building Department:

1. The north setback issue is real. On the Cultural Center, they were very strict about the ULDR (Unified Land Development Regulations) requirements. If you're proposing a step-back at floor 12, make sure you have a clear zoning analysis showing compliance. They'll want to see shadow studies too -- that caught us off guard on the Cultural Center.

2. They're currently requiring HVHZ (High-Velocity Hurricane Zone) compliance documentation as a separate submission package. This includes the NOA (Notice of Acceptance) for every exterior product -- windows, doors, facade panels, roofing. Start collecting these from manufacturers NOW. On the Cultural Center it took us 3 weeks just to get the NOA for the curtain wall system.

3. Fire separation between the parking podium and residential tower: they've been flagging this more aggressively lately. Make sure your rated assemblies are clearly documented, especially at the transition between the parking structure (Type I-A) and the residential floors (Type I-B, I assume?).

4. Accessibility review has been more detailed recently. They want to see the full accessible route from the public right-of-way through to every unit type, including all dimensions. It's not enough to just show the accessible unit layouts.

5. One more thing -- they now require a separate "Sustainability Compliance" form with the submission. It's a relatively new requirement (2024 code update). I can send you the blank form and our completed version from the Cultural Center as a reference.

Let me know if you want to sit down and go through the checklist together. I have about 45 minutes on Tuesday afternoon.

Keith
