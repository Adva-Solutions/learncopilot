Akshay,

Thanks for flagging this. The deeper elevator pit is a concern given the geotech findings. I reviewed the boring logs again this morning and the soft clay layer runs from roughly 28 to 35 feet. The 6'-6" pit puts the bottom of excavation right at 31 feet, which is squarely in that zone.

I see two options:

Option A: Deep auger-cast piles extending to 45 feet to reach bearing on the limestone layer. This adds approximately 3 weeks to the foundation schedule and an estimated $280,000 to $340,000 in additional cost.

Option B: Ground improvement using aggregate piers in the elevator pit area only. Faster by about 2 weeks compared to piles, estimated at $150,000 to $190,000, but requires a supplemental geotech investigation to confirm feasibility.

I have a meeting with the geotech engineer on Thursday to discuss both options. I'll have a recommendation by Friday. Can you copy David Okonkwo on this? He'll need the cost and schedule impact for the construction budget.
