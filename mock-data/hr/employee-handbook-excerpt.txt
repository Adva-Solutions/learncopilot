SECTION 1: PAID TIME OFF (PTO)
===============================================================================

1.1 PTO Accrual

ODA provides a combined PTO bank that covers vacation, personal days, and sick time.

Years of Service          Annual PTO Accrual     Monthly Accrual
0-2 years                 15 days (120 hours)    1.25 days/month
3-5 years                 20 days (160 hours)    1.67 days/month
6-10 years                25 days (200 hours)    2.08 days/month
10+ years                 30 days (240 hours)    2.50 days/month

PTO begins accruing on the first day of employment. New employees may use accrued PTO after completing 90 days of employment.

1.2 PTO Carryover
- Maximum carryover: 5 unused days may be carried into the following calendar year
- Carried-over days must be used by March 31 of the following year
- PTO days beyond the 5-day carryover limit are forfeited on December 31

1.3 PTO Request Process
- Requests of 1-2 days: Submit to your direct supervisor at least 3 business days in advance
- Requests of 3-5 days: Submit at least 2 weeks in advance
- Requests of 5+ days: Submit at least 4 weeks in advance
- All requests are subject to project schedule and team coverage requirements
- Requests during critical project milestones (CD issuance, permit submissions) may be deferred at the discretion of the project lead

1.4 Holidays
ODA observes the following paid holidays:
- New Year's Day
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day
- Presidents' Day
- Memorial Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Thanksgiving Day
- Day after Thanksgiving
- Christmas Eve (half day)
- Christmas Day
- New Year's Eve (half day)

Total: 12 paid holidays per year (including 2 half-days)

SECTION 2: REMOTE WORK POLICY (Updated Q4 2024)
===============================================================================

2.1 Standard Schedule
ODA operates on a hybrid work model:
- In-office days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (mandatory)
- Remote-eligible days: Monday, Friday
- Core hours (all days): 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM EST

2.2 Eligibility
- All full-time employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period
- Employees in the Fort Lauderdale office follow the same schedule
- Remote-only positions (approved by COO): Case-by-case basis

2.3 Remote Work Requirements
- Reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download)
- Functional webcam and microphone for video meetings
- Access to all required software (Revit, Rhino, etc. via office VPN or BIM 360)
- Responsiveness to Teams/email within 30 minutes during core hours
- Private workspace suitable for client calls and team meetings

2.4 Exceptions
- Project deadlines may require additional in-office days at the discretion of the project lead
- Client meetings and presentations are always in-office or on-site
- New employees during their first 90 days are expected to be in-office 5 days per week
- Design reviews and pin-ups are conducted in-office

SECTION 3: BENEFITS SUMMARY
===============================================================================

3.1 Health Insurance
Provider: Oxford (UnitedHealthcare)
Plans available:
- Oxford Liberty EPO: Employee only $85/month; Employee + Family $340/month
- Oxford Freedom POS: Employee only $120/month; Employee + Family $480/month
Enrollment: Within 30 days of hire date. Open enrollment in November annually.
Coverage effective: First of the month following enrollment completion.

3.2 Dental Insurance
Provider: MetLife
Coverage: Preventive 100%, Basic 80%, Major 50%
Annual maximum: $2,000 per person
Employee cost: $15/month (employee only); $45/month (family)

3.3 Vision Insurance
Provider: VSP
Coverage: Annual eye exam (copay $10), frames allowance $200, contact lens allowance $150
Employee cost: $8/month (employee only); $22/month (family)

3.4 401(k) Retirement Plan
Provider: Fidelity
Eligibility: After 6 months of employment
Company match: 50% of employee contribution up to 4% of salary (maximum 2% match)
Vesting: 3-year graded vesting (33% per year)

3.5 Professional Development
- AIA membership dues: Reimbursed for licensed architects
- ARE exam fees: Reimbursed upon passing (up to 2 attempts per division)
- Conference attendance: One conference per year (approved by supervisor), up to $2,000
- Continuing education: 18 AIA CEU credits per year supported through firm-sponsored sessions

3.6 Additional Benefits
- Life insurance: 1x annual salary (company-paid)
- Short-term disability: 60% of salary for up to 12 weeks
- Long-term disability: 60% of salary after 12 weeks (company-paid)
- Commuter benefits: Pre-tax transit and parking (up to IRS limits)
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP): 6 sessions per year (confidential)

SECTION 4: CODE OF CONDUCT (HIGHLIGHTS)
===============================================================================

4.1 Professional Standards
- All employees are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct
- Client confidentiality must be maintained at all times
- Project files, drawings, and design concepts are proprietary to ODA
- Use of personal AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) for client project work must follow the firm's AI Usage Policy (see Section 4.5)

4.2 Workplace Behavior
- ODA is committed to a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free workplace
- Discrimination based on race, gender, age, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected class will not be tolerated
- All complaints should be reported to HR Director Robert Flanagan or Senior HR Manager Joanna Regiec

4.3 Software and IT
- Only IT-approved software may be installed on ODA workstations
- Personal devices may connect to guest Wi-Fi only (not the firm network)
- All project files must be saved to the firm server, not local drives
- Revit models must follow the ODA BIM Standards Manual (available from Priya Narayanan)

4.4 Social Media
- Personal social media posts should not reference active client projects without Marketing approval
- Do not share project images, renderings, or drawings on personal accounts
- LinkedIn posts about ODA work are encouraged but should be reviewed by Lily Golabchi before posting

4.5 AI Usage Policy
- Enterprise-licensed AI tools (Microsoft Copilot) are approved for work use
- Personal AI tool subscriptions (ChatGPT, Claude) may be used for general research and email drafting
- Do NOT upload client contracts, financial data, or proprietary design files to personal AI tools
- For questions about appropriate AI usage, contact Diana Reyes (IT) or Megumi Takata (COO)

SECTION 5: LEAVE OF ABSENCE
===============================================================================

5.1 Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)
- Eligible after 12 months of employment and 1,250 hours worked
- Up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year
- Reasons: Birth/adoption of child, serious health condition of employee or family member
- Health insurance continues during FMLA leave

5.2 Parental Leave
- Birth parents: 8 weeks paid leave at 100% salary (in addition to any short-term disability)
- Non-birth parents: 4 weeks paid leave at 100% salary
- Additional unpaid leave available under FMLA

5.3 Bereavement Leave
- Immediate family: 5 paid days
- Extended family: 3 paid days

5.4 Jury Duty
- Paid leave for the duration of jury service
- Employee must provide documentation from the court
