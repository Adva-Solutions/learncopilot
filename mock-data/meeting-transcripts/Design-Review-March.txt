[Yinxing]: Alright everyone, thanks for joining. We have a lot to cover today on Shore Club. Let's start with the facade and then move through floor plans and permitting.

[Akshay]: Sure. So after the last round of client feedback, we explored three options. The first is the fluted precast concrete with bronze anodized aluminum accents. Second is the terracotta rain screen system. Third is a hybrid approach mixing the precast with terracotta on the upper residential floors.
[James]: From a structural standpoint, the terracotta rain screen adds about twelve percent more dead load on the curtain wall framing compared to the precast. It's manageable but it does affect the connection details at floors eight through fourteen.
[Akshay]: Right, and that's part of why we're leaning toward option three, the hybrid. We get the terracotta warmth on the residential tower where it matters visually, but keep the precast on the base and parking podium where we need durability and lower cost.
[Sarah]: I like the hybrid from an interiors perspective too. The terracotta tones on the upper floors connect nicely with the warm palette we're developing for the unit interiors. It gives us a coherent story from outside to inside.
[Yinxing]: What about the cost delta between option one and option three?
[Akshay]: We got preliminary numbers from two subcontractors. The hybrid is roughly eight to ten percent more than full precast, but about fifteen percent less than full terracotta. So it's the sweet spot.
[James]: I should mention the terracotta panels need a minimum six-inch cavity for the rain screen assembly. That eats into the floor plate on the upper levels by about eighteen inches per side.
[Yinxing]: Eighteen inches per side is significant. How does that affect unit square footage?
[Akshay]: We lose about forty to sixty square feet per unit on floors eight and above. It's not ideal but we can offset some of that by tightening the corridor widths on those floors. We're currently at six feet which is generous for residential.
[Sarah]: I wouldn't go below five foot six on the corridors. Below that it starts to feel tight, especially with the wall sconces we planned.
[Yinxing]: Agreed. Let's target five foot eight and see what that recovers. Akshay, can you run those numbers by Friday?
