[Rachel]: The architecture industry has shifted heavily toward video content and interactive project pages. A static gallery of photos isn't enough anymore. Prospective clients want to see process, context, and storytelling.
[Lily]: Exactly. I've been exploring a redesign with our web agency. The concept is a more editorial approach where each project page reads like a short magazine feature with narrative text, photography, process diagrams, and embedded video.
[Eran]: I like that direction. Our work deserves more context than just a photo grid. What's the timeline and budget?
[Lily]: The web agency estimates twelve weeks for the full redesign and build, with a budget of forty-five thousand. That includes the new project template system, updated navigation, and mobile optimization. We could launch by mid-July.
[Eran]: That's a meaningful investment. Can we phase it? Start with the project portfolio section and do the rest of the site in Q3?
[Lily]: Yes. Phase one would be the portfolio and homepage for about twenty-eight thousand. Phase two would be the team page, news section, and careers page. I think phasing makes sense.
[Rachel]: I'd also suggest adding a press and media section. When journalists cover your work they need quick access to high-resolution images and project facts. A proper media kit page saves everyone time.
[Lily]: Great point. I'll add that to phase one since it's relatively simple. Thalia, can you compile a list of all the press contacts we've worked with in the last two years? That'll help us design the media section.
[Thalia]: Sure. I have most of that in the PR tracker already. I'll clean it up and send it over.
[Lily]: Now let's talk social media strategy for Q2. Our Instagram has grown to about fourteen thousand followers but engagement has plateaued. LinkedIn is performing better with a thirty percent increase in engagement quarter over quarter.
[Rachel]: The architecture audience on LinkedIn is more engaged because the content tends to be more substantive. Project case studies, design thinking posts, and industry commentary perform well there. Instagram is more competitive and algorithm-dependent.
[Lily]: Nina has been doing great work on both platforms but we need a more structured content calendar. I'm proposing we move to a themed weekly schedule. Mondays are project spotlights, Wednesdays are behind-the-scenes or process content, and Fridays are team culture or office life posts.
[Eran]: I'd add a thought leadership component. Short posts where our principals share perspectives on industry trends, urban design issues, or sustainability. That positions us as experts, not just a portfolio showcase.
[Rachel]: Love that. We can repurpose those into a LinkedIn newsletter too. It builds a subscriber base that we own, separate from the algorithm.
[Lily]: Eran, would you be willing to do a monthly LinkedIn article? About eight hundred words on a topic of your choice?
[Eran]: Monthly is doable. I have strong opinions on the affordable housing design conversation happening right now. That could be the first one.
[Lily]: Perfect. Let's aim for the first article to go live in mid-April.
[Lily]: Last item is the RFP visual templates. We've been losing time on every proposal because the team starts from scratch with layouts and graphics each time.
[Lily]: Derek and I are developing a standardized RFP template system in InDesign. It'll include cover page options, team bio layouts, project sheets, and approach diagrams that can be customized for each submission.
[Eran]: This is overdue. How many hours do we typically spend on RFP graphics?
[Lily]: Based on the last six proposals, the average is about twenty-five hours of design time per RFP. With the template system, I think we can cut that to ten to twelve hours. That frees up Derek for other marketing work.
[Rachel]: Make sure the templates are flexible enough to feel custom. Nothing turns off a client faster than a proposal that looks like it was stamped out of a cookie cutter.
[Lily]: Absolutely. The system will have interchangeable modules and three distinct color palettes so each proposal can feel tailored. We're also building in placeholder content that guides the architects on what to write for each section.
[Eran]: When will the templates be ready?
[Lily]: First draft by April eleventh. I'd like to test them on the next RFP that comes in and iterate from there.
