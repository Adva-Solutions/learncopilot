AIA NEW YORK DESIGN AWARDS 2025
SUBMISSION NARRATIVE

PROJECT: Wynwood Arts Mixed-Use
FIRM: ODA Architecture
CATEGORY: Architecture - Mixed-Use

NARRATIVE (500 words)

The Wynwood Arts Mixed-Use building reimagines what a residential development can contribute to its neighborhood. Located in the heart of Miami's Wynwood Arts District -- a neighborhood defined by its creative energy, street art, and cultural entrepreneurship -- the project was designed not merely to occupy the site but to amplify the qualities that make Wynwood extraordinary.

ODA's concept of architectural porosity guided every design decision. At the ground level, the building's facade dissolves into a series of covered outdoor galleries and retail arcades that extend the neighborhood's vibrant street life directly into the building. There is no hard boundary between the sidewalk and the interior public spaces; instead, the transition is gradual, inviting, and animated by the art galleries, studios, and independent retailers that occupy the 35,000 square feet of ground-floor commercial space. This porosity is not merely aesthetic -- it is an economic strategy that supports Wynwood's creative ecosystem by providing affordable, flexible spaces for artists and small businesses that might otherwise be displaced by rising rents.

Above the public base, the 180-unit residential tower is organized around a central courtyard that brings natural light deep into the floor plate while creating a communal garden that serves as the building's social heart. The courtyard's tropical landscaping draws on Miami's subtropical ecology, incorporating native species that require minimal irrigation and contribute to the building's LEED Platinum certification.

The facade design establishes a dialogue between solid and void that is both formally striking and environmentally responsive. Precast concrete panels provide solar shading and thermal mass, while floor-to-ceiling glass panels frame views of the neighborhood and allow natural daylight to penetrate deep into the residential units. The material palette -- warm concrete, natural stone, and bronze-anodized aluminum -- ages gracefully in Miami's climate and references the industrial character of the neighborhood's converted warehouse buildings.

Sustainability was not an afterthought but a foundational design principle. The building achieved a 42% reduction in energy consumption compared to the ASHRAE 90.1 baseline through high-performance envelope design, efficient mechanical systems, and a building management system that optimizes energy use in real time. Over 60% of the structural concrete incorporates recycled content, and the green roof system on the amenity level reduces urban heat island effect while managing stormwater runoff. These measures earned the project LEED Platinum certification -- a distinction that demonstrates sustainability and design excellence are complementary, not competing, objectives.

The Wynwood Arts Mixed-Use building has been fully leased since opening, with a waiting list that underscores the market's appetite for design-driven residential experiences. More importantly, the ground-floor galleries and retail spaces are occupied entirely by local artists and independent businesses, fulfilling the project's promise to strengthen rather than supplant the creative community that defines Wynwood.

This project demonstrates ODA's conviction that architecture's highest purpose is to create places where individual aspiration and collective life reinforce one another -- where the quality of the building elevates not just the lives of its residents but the vitality of the neighborhood it inhabits.
