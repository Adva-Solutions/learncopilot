Intensity scale: 0 → 5+ voices Themes were synthesized from open-ended Q3 ("tedious") and Q4 ("hand off to AI") responses plus meeting excerpts.
Architecture
13 voices · 76% of survey
Challenges described
  • Reviewing submittals and RFIs against issued drawings and specs, "tedious due to cross-referencing between documents and tracking down specs." Technical Director (QA/QC & CA); Project Architect
  • Finding things: email threads, past projects, precedent files. "Whether we're drawing construction details or designing around a concept, an autofill should point us to the most relevant files ODA has previously done." Project Directors; Senior Architect; Marketing Manager
  • Building code and zoning compliance — "I'd offload building code analysis to AI." Named as the single task most worth handing off. Team Leader; Team Leader / Associate (FL)
  • Commenting on technical drawings and comparing the latest documents with previous versions. "Volume of markups, switching between tools, and repetitive comments make it tedious." Interior Principal; Team Leader / Associate (FL)
  • Visualization and rendering — iterating Rhino outputs through Nanobanana to reach presentation quality, with control and specificity the main friction. 5 respondents across project leadership
  • Cross-tool file-shuffling — moving PDFs between AutoCAD, Revit, Rhino, and InDesign to assemble presentations. Architecture designer
Agents that address these
CA Submittal Review Writer built in the workshop
Ingests a contractor submittal, cross-references against the referenced spec sections, and produces a structured review memo with item-level dispositions. Directly addresses the Technical Director's named pain.
Code & Zoning Compliance Checker
Reference-loaded with NYC zoning + FHA codes + ODA standard details. Upload a drawing or sketch and it flags the compliance questions a PA would otherwise chase manually.
ODA Server Librarian (precedent RAG)
Custom retrieval over the shared server's historical files: "show me every time we used Hammer Bronze on a lobby floor" returns the project, the detail, the photos. Solves the findability pain named across roles.
Drawing Version Diff Agent
Takes two versions of a CD set, produces an annotated diff by discipline — what moved, what was added, what got removed. Cuts the markup-comparison workflow dramatically.
Inbox Archivist
Runs nightly against Outlook: categorizes email threads by project + topic, files attachments to the project folder, surfaces stale action items. Addresses the top Project-Director pain.
HR
1 voice · Senior HR Manager
Challenges described
  • Scheduling and email back-and-forth — "the back-and-forth coordination and finding available times internally." Named as top tedious workflow
  • Recruiting, onboarding, payroll, benefits, visas — a broad mix of recurring-template outputs, each needing a calibrated response. Q2 "yesterday" answer spans this full list
  • Skepticism about AI outputs and handling sensitive data. The ask: "seeing specific examples relevant to my work and hands-on demos." Q7 blocker
Agents that address these
Onboarding Memo Writer built in the workshop
Hiring-manager intake form → structured 30/60/90 plan. Calibrated per level (junior / senior / director) and anchored on real projects named in the intake.
Interview Scheduling Assistant
Reads candidate email threads + checks internal Outlook availability, drafts time-slot proposals in HR's voice. Attacks the specific back-and-forth named as #1 tedious.
Policy Comms Drafter
Turns a policy update (parental leave extension; benefits survey results) into the firm-wide employee-facing memo, in ODA's HR voice.
Marketing
2 voices · Marketing Manager + Principal
Challenges described
  • Disorganized marketing server — "thousands of files, duplicate versions, outdated documents... for someone new, impossible to find anything in under 10 minutes." Worst-named pain across the entire survey. Marketing Manager
  • PR-agency coordination — "constant email back-and-forth to track deliverables, schedules, and requests" across multiple agency teams. Marketing Manager
  • Newsletter production — manual, imagery-heavy, time-consuming. Named as a top marketing pain during the intro call. Principal (intro call)
  • Manual task tracking — spreadsheets, Outlook To-Do, handwritten post-its. The Manager asked directly for a consolidated Asana / Monday / Deltek setup. Marketing Manager
Agents that address these
ODA Newsletter Writer built in the workshop
Monthly project-activity log → finished newsletter issue in ODA's voice, with imagery captions referenced from OpenAsset filenames. Directly targets the Principal's named pain.
Marketing Server Librarian
Custom RAG over the marketing server. "Find the lobby renders for Wynwood from 2024" returns the files with paths. Dissolves the 10-minute-per-search barrier the Manager named.
PR Coordination Assistant
Parses the weekly flow of PR-agency emails, extracts deliverables + deadlines + requests, publishes a rolling task list. Cuts the "constant back-and-forth" into a daily digest.
Press-Release Drafter
Project milestone (TCO, award, ribbon-cutting) → press-ready announcement draft, matched to Architectural Record / Dezeen / Metropolis editorial tone as needed.
Executive
4 voices · Principal, Executive Assistant, intro-call leaders
Challenges described
  • Travel itinerary preparation — "5 to 10 hours" per trip, producing 10–20-page PDFs with flights, locations, map directions, and LinkedIn bios of every attendee. Named independently by both the EA surveyed and the EA in the intro call. Executive Assistant
  • Email drafting at scale — the Principal and the EA both named inbox work as their #1 handoff candidate; the EA specifically asked for drafts that match her voice ("no long dashes"). Principal; Executive Assistant
  • Excel-based scheduling, staffing, and invoicing. "Manually built in Excel, and I'm motivated to find a more efficient way." Principal
  • Writing in the firm's voice — turning existing interviews, podcasts, and essays into consistent about-us and project copy. Named directly in the intro call as a high-value use. Founding Principal (intro call)
  • Proposal pricing — three fee-structure methods applied to size, program, typology. A repetitive, error-prone task the Principal explicitly wanted automated. Founding Principal (intro call)
Agents that address these
Travel Itinerary Writer built in the workshop
Forwarded booking confirmations + meeting RSVPs + attendee LinkedIns → polished 10–20 page itinerary. Collapses the EA's 5–10 hour task into a review pass.
Write Like Me (Principal voice) built in the workshop
Drafts emails, essays, and project write-ups in the Principal's voice. Seeded from his sent mail and published interviews so the voice is grounded in the real corpus.
Proposal Fee Generator
Size + program + typology → three fee options (percentage-of-construction, time-and-resources, fixed-fee) with comparisons to historical ODA contracts. Answers the intro-call ask directly.
Staffing & Schedule Builder
Reads the project portfolio's Excel staffing sheet, flags over- and under-allocation by studio, proposes re-balancing. Closes the gap the Principal named between his manual tracking and what he wants.
Weekly Proposals-Sent Briefing
Every Monday: a one-page digest of proposals sent last week — client, typology, fee, status. Named in the intro call as a Principal-morning ritual he'd like automated.
Operations
3 voices · Accounting + intro-call Operations Director
Challenges described
  • Contract review — "60-page contract, review for X, Y, Z that it's all matching within what we need." Named as the #1 low-hanging fruit in the intro call. Operations Director (intro call)
  • Invoicing — reimbursable expenses, duplicate-payment verification, and creating project invoices. "Waiting on information and manual data entry slow me down." Accounting lead
  • Excel ↔ QuickBooks reconciliation — vendor details and project data live in QB; reconciliation still runs through Excel exports. Accounting lead; intro call
  • Extracting construction-cost breakdowns from hundreds of historical requisitions — trade-by-trade percentages feeding design-for-budget decisions. Operations Director (intro call)
Agents that address these
Contract Review Writer built in the workshop
Client redlines or a full contract → structured review memo flagging deviations from ODA standard across scope, fee, liability, termination, insurance. Directly addresses the Ops Director's named #1.
Invoice Builder
Given the signed contract + phase-progress input, generates the month's invoice with correct reimbursables and flags duplicate-payment risks before it goes out.
Reconciliation Helper
Takes a QuickBooks export + the Excel reconciliation sheet, identifies vendor mismatches and posting-order variances, proposes the adjusting entries.
Requisition Cost Extractor
Point it at a folder of historical project requisitions and it returns trade-by-trade percentages of construction cost by typology. Feeds the design-for-budget conversation the Ops Director described.
A note on reading this. The chart and text describe the aggregate of what ODA staff said themselves, grouped by role rather than by name. Individual tagline and identity information has intentionally been held out. The five agents tagged built in the workshop are the ones students build by hand during the session; the remainder are follow-on opportunities that the same Report Writer, Researcher, and Write Like Me patterns can be recombined to produce.
