Agent Opportunity Analysis

Where Copilot earns its keep at ODA

Based on a synthesis of AI surveys and in-person meetings and interviews — grouped into per-department pain themes and the Copilot surfaces that directly address them.

Method. Responses from ODA staff across all five departments, complemented by in-person conversations with firm leadership.
Scope. Every-day admin workflows and recurring deliverables, with an eye to where AI would meaningfully compress time or improve consistency.
Solution mapping. Each department's challenges paired with the right Copilot surface — Notebook, Page, Copilot-in-App, Custom Agent, Custom Instructions, or Custom Development — depending on the shape of the task.

Architecture
13 voices · 76% of survey
Challenges described
  • Reviewing submittals and RFIs against issued drawings and specs, "tedious due to cross-referencing between documents and tracking down specs." Technical Director (QA/QC & CA); Project Architect
  • Finding things: email threads, past projects, precedent files. "Whether we're drawing construction details or designing around a concept, an autofill should point us to the most relevant files ODA has previously done." Project Directors; Senior Architect; Marketing Manager
  • Building code and zoning compliance — "I'd offload building code analysis to AI." Named as the single task most worth handing off. Team Leader; Team Leader / Associate (FL)
  • Commenting on technical drawings and comparing the latest documents with previous versions. "Volume of markups, switching between tools, and repetitive comments make it tedious." Interior Principal; Team Leader / Associate (FL)
  • Visualization and rendering — iterating Rhino outputs through Nanobanana to reach presentation quality, with control and specificity the main friction. 5 respondents across project leadership
  • Cross-tool file-shuffling — moving PDFs between AutoCAD, Revit, Rhino, and InDesign to assemble presentations. Architecture designer
Copilot solutions
Submittal Spec Notebook Notebook
Project spec sections loaded as Notebook knowledge. Drop in a submittal package and ask "does this cover every attribute required by spec 08 4413?" — Copilot answers with section-by-section citations. Solves the cross-referencing pain directly without building anything custom.
Code & Zoning Notebook Notebook
NYC zoning, NYC building code, FHA, and ODA's standard detail library loaded once. Any PA asks questions like "what's the required egress width for this occupancy?" and gets grounded answers with citations — the blocker named across multiple team leaders.
Precedent Library (indexed server) Custom Development
The marketing+project server is far too large for a Notebook. A custom-built indexed retrieval layer (embeddings over files, metadata by project/material/typology) lets any staffer ask "show me every Hammer Bronze lobby floor we've done" — answered in seconds instead of ten minutes per folder crawl.
Inbox Archivist Copilot in Outlook Custom Instructions
Native Copilot in Outlook handles categorization, thread summary, and attachment filing. A set of ODA-specific Custom Instructions (project-code patterns, consultant names, archive rules) makes the categorization fit the firm's actual folder structure.
Drawing Version Diff Custom Development
A CD-set diff tool integrated with Bluebeam — needs custom work because visual-diff over drawings isn't a native Copilot capability. Copilot can then interpret and narrate the diff by discipline, surfacing the non-trivial changes.
HR
1 voice · Senior HR Manager
Challenges described
  • Scheduling and email back-and-forth — "the back-and-forth coordination and finding available times internally." Named as top tedious workflow
  • Recruiting, onboarding, payroll, benefits, visas — a broad mix of recurring-template outputs, each needing a calibrated response. Q2 "yesterday" answer spans this full list
  • Skepticism about AI outputs and handling sensitive data. The ask: "seeing specific examples relevant to my work and hands-on demos." Q7 blocker
Copilot solutions
Interview Scheduling Copilot in Outlook
Native Copilot in Outlook reads the candidate thread, checks internal availability across the interview panel, and drafts the proposal email in HR's voice. The #1 tedious workflow collapses into one review step.
Benefits & Policies Notebook Notebook
Full policy corpus loaded once — handbook, benefits guides, compliance docs, leave policies. Managers self-serve the routine "what's our parental leave policy?" questions instead of routing them to HR, while HR retains control over sensitive casework.
Policy Comms Drafter Page
Pages are the right fit for co-authored memos that go firm-wide: HR drafts, a partner reviews inline, People Team finalizes, one link distributes. Turns a policy change into a clean employee-facing announcement in one collaborative flow.
Marketing
2 voices · Marketing Manager + Principal
Challenges described
  • Disorganized marketing server — "thousands of files, duplicate versions, outdated documents... for someone new, impossible to find anything in under 10 minutes." Worst-named pain across the entire survey. Marketing Manager
  • PR-agency coordination — "constant email back-and-forth to track deliverables, schedules, and requests" across multiple agency teams. Marketing Manager
  • Newsletter production — manual, imagery-heavy, time-consuming. Named as a top marketing pain by firm leadership. Founding Principal
  • Manual task tracking — spreadsheets, Outlook To-Do, handwritten post-its. The Manager asked directly for a consolidated Asana / Monday / Deltek setup. Marketing Manager
Copilot solutions
Indexed Image & Asset Library Custom Development
The named pain — thousands of files with no structure — cannot be solved by any Copilot surface alone. Requires building an indexed database (on top of OpenAsset, as proposed) with embeddings over image metadata and project context. Once indexed, Copilot becomes the conversational front door: "find all the lobby renders for Wynwood from 2024" in seconds.
Voice & Style Notebook Notebook
Past approved press releases, AIA jury submissions, newsletter archives, and the firm's core brand-voice guidelines loaded together. Any staffer writing external-facing copy works from this — consistency without a bottleneck through the Marketing Manager.
Press-Release Drafter Page
Milestone (TCO, award, ribbon-cutting) → a Copilot Page where Marketing drafts, a Principal reviews, PR agency adds their edits, all in one link. Faster than email back-and-forth and leaves an audit trail.
PR Coordination Digest Custom Agent
Parses the weekly flow of PR-agency emails, extracts deliverables + deadlines + requests, publishes a rolling task list. An agent (not a Notebook) because the task is inbox-parsing, not knowledge-grounded answering.
Executive
4 voices · Principal, Executive Assistant, intro-call leaders
Challenges described
  • Travel itinerary preparation — "5 to 10 hours" per trip, producing 10–20-page PDFs with flights, locations, map directions, and LinkedIn bios of every attendee. Named independently from two different executive-support voices. Executive Assistant
  • Email drafting at scale — the Principal and the EA both named inbox work as their #1 handoff candidate; the EA specifically asked for drafts that match her voice ("no long dashes"). Principal; Executive Assistant
  • Excel-based scheduling, staffing, and invoicing. "Manually built in Excel, and I'm motivated to find a more efficient way." Principal
  • Writing in the firm's voice — turning existing interviews, podcasts, and essays into consistent about-us and project copy. Named directly as a high-value use. Founding Principal
  • Proposal pricing — three fee-structure methods applied to size, program, typology. A repetitive, error-prone task the Principal explicitly wanted automated. Founding Principal
Copilot solutions
Firm Voice Notebook Notebook
Past interviews, podcast transcripts, published essays, and hero project-descriptions loaded as grounded knowledge. When the Principal — or any designer — writes about-us copy, jury submissions, or interview answers, the Notebook keeps the firm's voice consistent without a manual rewrite.
Inbox Triage Copilot in Outlook Custom Instructions
Native Outlook Copilot handles draft + summarize + prioritize. Custom Instructions tuned to each Principal's voice ("no long dashes," specific sign-offs, preferred formality) turn the drafts into something actually sendable without manual rewrite.
Proposal Fee Model Copilot in Excel + Notebook
Excel's native Copilot drives the model (size + program + typology → fee options). A paired methodology Notebook holds the three pricing frameworks and historical contract benchmarks so the model stays grounded in how ODA actually prices.
Staffing & Schedule Copilot in Excel
The Principal's Excel staffing sheet is the right surface — Copilot in Excel analyzes allocation per studio, flags over/under, and proposes re-balancing inline. No need to rebuild into a new tool; just bring AI to the existing workflow.
Weekly Proposals-Sent Briefing Custom Agent
Every Monday: a one-page digest of proposals sent last week — client, typology, fee, status. Named as a Principal-morning ritual worth automating; an agent (not a Notebook) because the task is aggregation + structured output.
Travel Itinerary Writer Custom Agent
Forwarded booking confirmations + meeting RSVPs + attendee LinkedIns → polished 10–20-page itinerary. The 5–10-hour EA task collapses into a review pass.
Operations
3 voices · Accounting + intro-call Operations Director
Challenges described
  • Contract review — "60-page contract, review for X, Y, Z that it's all matching within what we need." Named as the #1 low-hanging fruit. Operations Director
  • Invoicing — reimbursable expenses, duplicate-payment verification, and creating project invoices. "Waiting on information and manual data entry slow me down." Accounting lead
  • Excel ↔ QuickBooks reconciliation — vendor details and project data live in QB; reconciliation still runs through Excel exports. Accounting lead; Operations Director
  • Extracting construction-cost breakdowns from hundreds of historical requisitions — trade-by-trade percentages feeding design-for-budget decisions. Operations Director
Copilot solutions
Contract Terms Notebook Notebook
ODA standard contract terms, past approved redlines, insurance requirements, and precedent memos loaded together. Before counter-redlining, ask "what's our standard indemnification language?" and get a grounded answer. Dissolves the looking-it-up time that slows every review.
Contract Review Writer Custom Agent
Client redlines → structured review memo flagging every deviation from ODA standard across scope, fee, liability, termination, insurance. An agent is right here because the output is a templated recurring document with strict structure.
Invoicing & Reconciliation Copilot in Excel
Cash flow, vendor details, and reimbursable invoicing already live in Excel alongside QuickBooks. Copilot in Excel handles the in-spreadsheet drafting, duplicate-payment checking, and reconciliation narratives natively — no new tool to adopt.
QuickBooks Integration Custom Development
To fully close the loop between email-invoices-to-QB data entry and the Excel reconciliation layer, a QuickBooks MCP (or equivalent direct integration) is needed. Allows the accounting lead to tell Copilot "create invoice for 412 Nostrand April phase-complete" and have it land in QB.
Requisition Cost Index Custom Development
Hundreds of past requisition PDFs — too many for a Notebook. A custom-indexed retrieval layer extracts trade-by-trade cost breakdowns, then Copilot queries across typology and time to feed the design-for-budget conversation.
A note on reading this. The chart and text describe the aggregate of what ODA staff said themselves, grouped by role rather than by name. Individual identity information has intentionally been held out. Each solution listed is matched to the Copilot surface best suited to the shape of the pain — grounded knowledge goes into Notebooks, co-authored drafts into Pages, in-app workflows stay in their app, structured recurring deliverables become Agents, and anything requiring indexed infrastructure (large servers, image libraries, historical PDF corpora) gets flagged as custom development.
